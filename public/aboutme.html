About Me

I love cats, tacos, and Jeremy Renner.
I am from Chicago, IL and grew up in the country. A small town called Spring Valley.
For the past 24 years I have lived in Texas. I currently live in the town of Mission.
I went to Junior College to be a chef but ended up very ill and unable to finish schooling.
However, I worked as a chef at a hotel before I moved into retail.
I have also worked at an Eco-dry cleaner as an asst general manager for a few years.
Currently I am a secretary for a local church.
I suffer from depression, anxiety and bipolar. I have been this way most of my life.
When I was a child, my father beat my mother, and it left a terrible scar on my life.
I do not have actual memories of it, but I think it caused some PTSD. My mom left him when
I was two, and I never had a good relationship with him.
When I was younger, I suffered from low self-esteem. I was bullied about my weight, and about
my clothes which my mom made most of. At that time there were no plus-sized clothing for people.
Though I did have friends, I did not have a lot of them. Most of the time I felt like I stood out,
and not in a good way.
I do have some good memories from childhood, but I was fearful, worried and sad, and overate my worries,
fear and sadness. When I was 18 years old, I tried to kill myself. I took over 500 Advil and ended
up in ICU getting my stomach pumped. The psychiatrist saw me but said there was nothing wrong with me!
He sent me home without any kind of treatment. My depression really took off after that.
I also suffered from female problems that left me with heavy, long lasting, and painful periods.
I had countless cysts that ruptured, and it was terrible. The hormonal imbalance added to my depression.
I was unable to hold down a job or finish Culinary School. I kept missing days due to my conditions,
and it left me feeling worthless. In May of 2012 I felt extremely suicidal again. By this time, I was on
and off depression medication, and I needed help. I called the Suicide Hotline, and the police came to
my apartment. I was able to get some help after that.
I had terrible swings of emotions, and found myself with depression mostly, but sometimes I would be so
happy I would be making bad decisions. Decisions like spending too much money and drinking too much.
I would feel out of control during those happy times.
I decided to give up my apartment and move in with my mom. I was unable to care for myself and I could
barely care for my cats. I hired a lawyer and got on disability. I was able to see psychiatrists and
get the help I truly needed. That was when I was diagnosed with bipolar.
Even after treatment, I did still feel suicidal. I entered a mental health unit and stayed for two weeks.
This is when my migraines went out of control. I was having migraines that lasted for weeks at a time.
I could not function. The migraines did not get better, and I ended up not being able to work for 12 years.
I did pay my mom some of my disability money for “rent” and it made me feel like I was contributing.
The migraines became so bad that they were lasting for months. No kind of treatment helped them. Some
of the most common migraine medicines I could not take, and the others simply did not work. I tried Botox,
acupuncture, cold packs, medication, and I was in the hospital constantly.
This all made my depression, anxiety and bipolar worse. I was to the point where I could not get out of bed.
The one thing that saved me were my cats. I could not let my cats down. I had to be there for them. I would
get up and feed them and clean their litter box, and they stuck by me through it all. Whenever I felt like giving
up, I would think about my cats and cancel any plans for killing myself.
In late 2019 my migraines changed into complex migraines. At least once a month I was having a migraine that
acted like a stroke. I would pass out and not remember anything. I would not be able to walk and had to stay
in the hospital for a week at a time. It took me several weeks to regain complete control only to have it happen again.
I seriously stayed in bed all day and night. I got up to take care of my cats, but I was not taking care of myself.
And I was hardly eating.
One time my discharge papers had comments on them about migraines and foods you should avoid. My mom read through it
and did some research. It turns out that 90% of the foods I was eating were considered bad for migraines. Even good for
you foods like avocados and healthy yogurt. We made the big decision for me to stop eating all those foods. It was
very hard, and I was depressed over it.
I continued to stay in bed with my old routine. However, little by little the complex migraines stopped and eventually
all my headaches went away. I felt like a new person. Several months later, my depression, anxiety and bipolar improved.
The medication I was on was working, and I was off the numerous medications for migraines. The many side effects went away.
I was able to start a part-time job and earn a little more money. I got a new computer and got back on the internet and social
media. I still have depression, anxiety and bipolar, but I can handle it much better.
The reason I am sharing all of this is because your mental health is important. If you feel suicidal, don’t hesitate
to call for help. The national hotline number is 988 (United States of America). For a list of hotlines in other countries,
check out this link: List of Suicide Crisis Lines
